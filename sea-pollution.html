Sea Pollution & Marine Life

Raising awareness about water pollution and its effects on marine life.

Problem

Every year, 8 million tons of plastic enter the ocean. Once in the ocean, the harsh conditions and constant motion cause plastic to break down into microplastics. Making it impossible to retrieve! The microplastics then get further and deeper into the ocean affective marine life and its habitats.

A 2022 study found that 75% of the fish we eat contain microplastics, highlighting the urgency to address the issue of plastic waste.

Solution

I chose a sardine tin as my medium to raise awareness about plastic pollution in our oceans and its devastating effects on marine life.

The fish graphic on the tin appears as a regular fish at first glance, but upon closer inspection, it becomes evident that the fish is entirely composed of discarded plastic commonly found in our oceans. My aim was to raise awareness and draw attention to this critical issue by pairing this striking image with provocative text.
